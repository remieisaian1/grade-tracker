Frequently asked questions

Find answers about university grade calculations, credits, year weighting, degree classifications, privacy, and how to use Degree Grade Tracker.

How do I calculate my university grade?

First, calculate each module mark using the assessment weights. Then multiply each module mark by its credits, add those results together, and divide by the total credits. This gives your credit-weighted year average. Your final degree estimate is then calculated using the year weighting.

What is a credit-weighted average?

A credit-weighted average means larger modules count more towards your overall average. For example, a 30-credit module counts twice as much as a 15-credit module.

What are module credits?

Credits show the size or value of a module. A module with more credits has more impact on your year average. If you do not know your credits, you can keep the same credit value for every module so they are treated equally.

Do Year 2 and Year 3 count towards my final degree?

Many UK universities use Year 2 and Year 3 to calculate the final degree classification, but the exact weighting depends on the university and course. Some universities may use 25% for Year 2 and 75% for Year 3, while others may use 20% and 80%, or another method.

Can I change the Year 2 and Year 3 weighting?

Yes. Degree Grade Tracker lets you manually change the Year 2 and Year 3 weighting. When you change one year, the other updates automatically so the total stays at 100%.

What grade do I need for a First?

A First Class degree is usually awarded for a final average of 70% or above. However, universities may also use extra rules for rounding, credits, borderline marks, or final-year performance.

What grade do I need for a 2:1?

A 2:1, also called an Upper Second Class degree, is usually awarded for a final average of 60% to 69%. Some universities may have borderline rules if you are close to the next classification.

Does this calculator work for every university?

It can be used as a general estimate because you can change module credits and year weightings manually. However, every university can have different regulations, so you should always check your official course handbook or academic regulations.

Why might my real university result be different?

Your official result may be different because universities can use extra rules such as dropped credits, best-credit calculations, rounding rules, compensation, resit caps, and borderline classification rules.

Are my grades saved online?

No. Your grades are saved only in your own browser using local storage. Your module names, credits, assessment weights, and marks are not sent to a server.

Can I clear my saved grades?

Yes. Use the “clear all” button on the calculator page. This removes the saved data from your browser.

Is Degree Grade Tracker official?

No. Degree Grade Tracker is an independent student tool. It is designed to help you estimate your grade, but your official result will always come from your university.
