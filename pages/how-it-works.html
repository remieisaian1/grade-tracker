How to calculate your university grade

This page explains how to enter your modules, credits, assessment weights, and year weightings to estimate your final degree grade.

1. Add your modules

Start by adding the modules you are taking in Year 2 and Year 3. A module could be something like Web Development, Databases, Networking, Final Year Project, or Dissertation.

2. Enter module credits

Credits show how large a module is. A 30-credit module counts twice as much as a 15-credit module when calculating your year average.

Example:

Module A: 70% with 30 credits

Module B: 60% with 15 credits

Module A will have more impact because it has more credits.

3. Add assessments, weights, and grades

Inside each module, add your assessments. For example, a module might have coursework worth 40% and an exam worth 60%.

Module grade = assessment mark × assessment weight

Example: if coursework is 70% and worth 40%, and the exam is 60% and worth 60%:

Module grade = (70 × 0.4) + (60 × 0.6) = 64%

4. Understand credit-weighted averages

Your year average is calculated using module credits. This means bigger modules affect your year average more.

Year average = sum(module mark × module credits) ÷ total credits

5. Adjust year weighting

Different universities use different year weightings. Some may use 25% for Year 2 and 75% for Year 3, while others may use 20% and 80%, or another method.

The calculator lets you manually change the year weighting so it can fit more universities.

Important note

This calculator gives an estimate only. Universities may use different rules for rounding, credits, compensation, resits, borderline classifications, or dropped credits. Always check your official university regulations.
